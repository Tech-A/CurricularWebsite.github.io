The Arts



What is it?

At Marist College, students have opportunities to grow their confidence and skills through the arts. Marist College offers a range of performing arts and art opportunities to help nurture creativity.




Benefits

Students will learn to express their creativity and be able to explore their skills and abilities.




Extracurriculars under the arts

Drawing club

Students can draw, paint or express their creativity on a range of mediums (eg. paper), individually or with the guidance of an art teacher. Takes place in breaks in the art room (V208).


Suggestions:

  • Paints, paintbrushes etc (though these can be borrowed from the art department)
  • Paper/visual diary




Improv group

A group where students can learn the basics of improvisation and try it out.


No talents or skills required. Just come with a willing, enthusiastic mindset.





Musical production

Every year, Marist College, usually with a brothering school (St Peters) presents a musical production for students with musical talent and interest to take part in. In 2021, Marist College and St Peters presented the Sound of Music. Next year, in 2022, Marist College and St Peters will hold Grease the musical. Auditions for this take place in term 4. Email details at the bottom of the page.




Cultural group

Every year, Marist College holds a family festival, showcasing different cultures. Students can join cultural groups and perform cultural dances at the festival. Some groups are even taken to Polyfest. Sign up sheets are available at the start of next year, a few weeks before the festival.
